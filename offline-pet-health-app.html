Most pet apps quietly assume you're online: they want an account, they sync to a cloud, and some features stall without a connection. If your priority is "works anywhere, owes nothing to a server, and keeps my pet's history private," that's a narrower set of apps. This page goes deep on exactly that — the offline situations where it matters, and what "private and on-device" really means here.

When offline isn't a nice-to-have — it's the whole point

The vet's exam room

Concrete walls and lower-level rooms eat cell signal. That's the exact moment you need to show the vet the last vaccine date, current meds, or a weight from three months ago. An offline app just opens the record — no spinner, no "no connection" message.

Rural homes and properties

Plenty of homes, farms and acreages have patchy or no mobile data. A records app that needs the internet to load your own pet's history is useless there. GentlePet doesn't phone home to work.

Travel and crossing borders

Roaming off, airplane mode on, a SIM you haven't swapped yet — travel is full of moments with no data. If you're moving an animal, boarding it, or visiting a vet abroad, the records need to be available from the device itself.

Emergencies and dead zones

When something's wrong you don't want to be fighting a login screen or a slow sync. Local-first means the medications list, allergies and microchip ID are accessible immediately, connection or not.

"Offline" and "private" are the same decision here

Because GentlePet keeps everything on the device, there's no account to create and no cloud copy of your pet's medical history sitting somewhere else. That's not a setting you toggle — it's how the app is built.

What that means in practice:
  • No account — you never sign up, log in, or hand over an email to use it.
  • No cloud storage — records aren't uploaded; they stay in the app on your phone.
  • Play Data safety: no data collected, no data shared — the listing states the app collects no data and shares none.
  • Nothing to leak — there's no server-side database of your pet's history to be breached or sold.
  • You own the export — when you want to share, you generate a PDF medical card yourself and send it however you choose.

One honest trade-off worth knowing: because data lives only on your device and isn't backed up to a cloud, keeping your own device backup is on you. That's the cost of nothing being uploaded — and for privacy-minded owners, it's usually the point.

Offline & privacy questions

Does GentlePet work with no internet?

Yes — it's designed to run fully offline. You can use it in airplane mode or anywhere with no signal; your pet's records load from your device.

Do I have to create an account?

No. There's no sign-up and no login. You install it and start keeping records.

Where is my pet's data stored?

On your phone. Per the Google Play Data safety section, the app collects no data and shares no data.

If there's no cloud, how do I share records with my vet?

You export a PDF medical card from the app and share that file yourself — email, messaging, or print. Nothing is uploaded automatically.

What happens to my records if I'm offline for weeks?

Nothing changes — everything keeps working, because the app never depended on a connection in the first place.

Is it really free?

There's a free tier (1 pet, up to 30 photos, 5 reminders). Premium is a one-time $4.99 purchase — no subscription.
