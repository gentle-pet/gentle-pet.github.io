If you searched for a "PetDesk alternative," a "pet records app not tied to a clinic," or an "offline pet app," you're usually looking for one specific shift: from a clinic-connected service to records you keep yourself.

PetDesk and GentlePet aren't the same kind of tool

This isn't really a "which is better" question — it's a "which job do you need done" question. They overlap in name (both touch pet health) but aim at different things.

PetDesk — the general model

A clinic-oriented, account-based service. Its strength is the link to a participating veterinary practice: booking appointments, getting clinic reminders, and messaging through the clinic. Useful when your vet uses it and you want that channel.

GentlePet — the general model

An owner-owned, offline record-keeper. No clinic link, no account, no cloud. You record your pet's history yourself and it stays on your phone. Strength: independence, privacy, and a portable PDF you can take anywhere.

When a clinic-independent records app makes more sense

People move from a clinic-connected app to an owner-owned one for reasons like:

  • You changed vets, moved cities, or use more than one clinic. Records tied to one practice don't follow you cleanly; records on your own phone do.
  • Your vet doesn't use that platform. A clinic-connected app's best features need a participating clinic — without one, you mostly get an account you didn't want.
  • You want it to work with no signal. Travelling, rural areas, a clinic basement — an offline app opens instantly.
  • You'd rather not have a login or cloud account for your pet's medical history.
  • You want a file you control — a PDF medical card to email, print, or bring to any vet, including a new one.

What you can keep in GentlePet

One private place, on your own phone, for each pet:

  • Vaccinations and deworming history
  • Vet visits and notes
  • Weight over time
  • Allergies and medications
  • Reminders you set yourself
  • Photos, microchip ID, and pedigree
  • A PDF medical-card export to share with any vet

Free tier: 1 pet, 30 photos, 5 reminders. One-time $4.99 Premium adds multiple pets (no per-pet paywall) and removes those limits. App is ~11 MB and available in 60+ languages.

Who should pick which

  • Stick with a clinic-connected app if your vet uses it and you mainly want appointment booking, clinic reminders, and messaging through that practice.
  • Choose GentlePet if you want a private, offline record book you own — independent of any clinic, with no account and no subscription, and an exportable PDF for whichever vet you see.

Honest note: GentlePet is a young, focused app and intentionally does not replace a clinic's booking or messaging system. It complements it — keep using your clinic's tools for appointments, and use GentlePet as your own copy of the records.

Frequently asked questions

Is GentlePet a direct replacement for PetDesk?

Not exactly — they do different jobs. PetDesk's general model centers on connecting you to a clinic; GentlePet is a personal, offline records keeper that isn't tied to any clinic. Many people use a clinic's own tools for booking and GentlePet for their own records.

I want a pet records app that isn't tied to a clinic — is that GentlePet?

Yes. GentlePet works with any vet or none. Your records live on your phone, so they aren't connected to a single practice and move with you if you change vets.

Does it really work offline and without an account?

Yes — GentlePet works fully offline and requires no account. You can add and view records with no internet connection.

Can I share records with my vet?

Yes — you can export a PDF medical card and share or print it for any vet. GentlePet doesn't message or book through a clinic; it gives you a file you control.

Is there a subscription?

No. There's a free tier and a one-time $4.99 Premium purchase — no monthly or yearly fees.

Is my pet's data private?

Per its Google Play Data safety section, GentlePet collects no data and shares no data. Records are stored locally on your device.
