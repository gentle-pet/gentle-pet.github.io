The moment is familiar: the kennel confirms your booking "as long as vaccinations are up to date," the groomer asks for proof at the door, daycare wants records on file, or the airline desk asks for documentation. The information usually exists — your vet gave it to you — it's just on a paper slip in a drawer, a photo lost in your camera roll, or in an email from eighteen months ago. This page is about using GentlePet to keep that information in one place so you can produce it on the spot.

Keep the records the facility will ask you to hand over

You don't decide what's required — your vet and the facility do. What you can do is keep the records you already have organized and exportable. Here's what GentlePet lets you write down and carry forward.

Vaccination dates from your vet's records

Enter the vaccinations your pet has had and the dates exactly as they appear on the record your veterinarian gave you. The app stores what you type — it does not tell you which shots are needed or whether they're "current." That's between your vet and the facility. Keeping the dates in one place just means you can answer "when was the last one?" without digging.

A PDF medical card to email or print

Export a PDF medical card summarizing what you've recorded, then email it ahead to the kennel, print it for the groomer, or show it at a counter. It's your own records export — a tidy copy of what you entered, ready to share. Whether the facility accepts it as proof is their call (more on that below).

Microchip ID and your pet's basics

Keep the microchip number, breed, date of birth and weight on hand — the details a boarding form, daycare intake or travel desk often asks for alongside vaccinations. One screen instead of three documents.

Vet-visit history and a clinic to call

A dated record of visits, plus space for notes, means that if a facility wants to verify something with your vet, you have the context and the visit history in front of you instead of trying to remember.

Photos of the original paperwork

Attach a photo of the actual vaccination certificate or record card your vet issued. The typed dates make a quick summary; the photo of the original is what some facilities really want to see. Both live on your phone, offline.

The drop-off scramble, minus the scramble

Here's the practical sequence GentlePet is built for — keeping records, not advising on them.

1. Enter it once, from the record you already have

When you get back from a vet visit, type in the vaccination dates and snap a photo of the certificate. Five minutes, once, instead of a search every time something asks for it.

2. Confirm what the facility actually needs

Ask the kennel, groomer, daycare, dog park, airline or destination what they require — GentlePet won't tell you, and shouldn't. Requirements differ by place and change over time. This step is on you and your vet.

3. Export the PDF and hand it over

Generate the PDF medical card and email it ahead, print it, or show it at drop-off. Because everything is stored on the phone, you can do this in the car park with no signal.

  • You hold the copy — the records are on your device, not in someone else's account, and you choose when to share the PDF.

Boarding & travel record-keeping questions

Does GentlePet tell me which vaccinations my pet needs for boarding or travel?

No. GentlePet does not provide vaccine requirements, schedules or due dates, and it doesn't know any facility's or country's rules. It only keeps the records your vet gave you. Ask your veterinarian and the specific kennel, daycare, airline or destination what they require.

Can I email or print my pet's records for a kennel or groomer?

Yes — export a PDF medical card from what you've recorded and email it ahead or print it. It's a copy of the information you entered, organized in one document.

Is the PDF an official pet passport or accepted document?

No. It is your own records export, not an official passport, health certificate or government document. Whether a facility or authority accepts it is entirely their decision — many require official paperwork issued or sent by your vet, so always check first.

Will it tell me if my pet is "up to date"?

No. GentlePet stores the dates you enter but does not judge whether vaccinations are current — that depends on rules it doesn't set. Your vet and the facility decide that.

Can I keep records for more than one pet going to boarding?

Yes — Premium covers multiple pets with no per-pet paywall, as a single one-time purchase, so the whole household's records are in one app.

Does it work without internet at the kennel or airport?

Yes. GentlePet is fully offline with no account, so your records and the PDF export are available even with no signal.

Is this veterinary advice?

No. GentlePet is a record-keeping tool only. It does not diagnose, treat, advise, or tell you what any facility or country requires. Always consult your veterinarian for medical decisions and confirm requirements with the facility, airline or destination.

What does it cost?

There's a free tier (1 pet, up to 30 photos, 5 reminders). Premium is a one-time $4.99 purchase with no subscription.
