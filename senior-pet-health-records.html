Keep the records your vet's plan needs

Your veterinarian sets the plan. Your job between visits is often just to keep good notes. Here's what GentlePet lets you write down and carry forward.

The medications list (as your vet prescribed)

Record the medications your pet is on so you have one current, accurate list — useful when a vet or an emergency clinic asks "what is she taking?" The app stores what you enter; it does not suggest, schedule or dose anything. Follow your vet's instructions for what and how much.

Weight over time

Log each weigh-in to build a simple history you can show the vet. Weight is something many care plans track, and a tidy record over months is more useful than a number you half-remember. What the trend means is a conversation for your vet.

Vet-visit history

Keep a dated record of visits and your own notes — what was discussed, what changed. Over a year of appointments that history becomes the backbone of the conversation at the next one.

Reminders you've been asked to keep

Set reminders for the things your vet asked you to do — a recheck, a refill, a regular task. You decide the dates based on your vet's guidance; the app simply reminds you. It won't tell you when something is "due" on its own.

Allergies, microchip ID and a PDF to hand over

Note known allergies and the microchip ID, then export a PDF medical card to bring or send to the clinic — so the vet sees an organized summary instead of a scramble of memories.

Why a longitudinal record helps at the vet

  • One source instead of memory — meds, weights and dates in one place you can pull up at the counter.
  • Easier handoffs — a new vet, a specialist, or an emergency clinic can be handed a PDF instead of pieced-together recollections.
  • Continuity across visits — you can see what you recorded last time before you walk in.
  • Less mental load — caring for a senior pet is enough work; the record-keeping shouldn't live in your head.

Built to stay simple and private

GentlePet works fully offline with no account, so the record is on your phone and ready in the exam room even with no signal. Senior & chronic-care record-keeping questions

Does the app tell me when vaccines or medications are due?

No. GentlePet does not provide schedules, dosages or due dates. You set your own reminders based on what your veterinarian tells you, and the app reminds you on those dates.

Can it track multiple medications for one pet?

You can record the medications your pet is on so you keep one accurate list. The app stores what you enter — it doesn't recommend, schedule or calculate anything.

How does it help with weight?

You log each weigh-in and it keeps the history so you can show the trend to your vet. Whether the trend is good or concerning is for your vet to judge.

Can I give the records to a new or emergency vet?

Yes — export a PDF medical card and bring or send it, so the clinic sees an organized history of meds, weights, visits and allergies.

Is this medical or veterinary advice?

No. GentlePet is a record-keeping tool only. It does not diagnose, treat or advise. Always consult your veterinarian for medical decisions.

What does it cost?

There's a free tier (1 pet, up to 30 photos, 5 reminders). Premium is a one-time $4.99 purchase with no subscription.
